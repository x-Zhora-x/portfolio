Random Walk Simulator

This project simulates the behavior of ants as they explore. The ants take a number of steps in a direction before randomly switching to a new direction, mimicking natural foraging behavior. Once they locate food, they walk a straight line home. This model showcases how such simple, rule-based movements can lead to efficient foraging patterns.

Kalshi Election Correlation Time Series
(Work In Progress)

I'm planning to use Cholesky decomposition and time series analysis to explore correlations between swing states in U.S. elections. The goal is to uncover patterns in how these states' results move together, which will(in theory)(allegedly) allow me to predict which ones are likely to "swing" in the same direction. This could reveal mispriced markets on Kalshi or Polymarket. I'll start by analyzing historical election data, build a covariance matrix, and run Monte Carlo simulations to model possible election outcomes. This approach should help me spot betting odds that don’t match the actual risks.
